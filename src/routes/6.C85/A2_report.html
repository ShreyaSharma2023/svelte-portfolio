How well has the communities act been followed in Massachusetts?

I found the MBTA communities act to be a good starting point for this analysis. One of the requirements of this act is that an MBTA community should have atleast one district in which multi family housing is permitted which is not more than 0.5 miles away from a transit station.

The first step was to filter all the municipalities by whether they are an MBTA community. Out of the 101 municipalities listed here, four were non listed as MBTA communities, namely: Bolton, Boston, Hudson, and Milford. Out of those Bolton and Milford were only single family households.

I then filtered by 'only single families' to look at municipalities where only single family zoning was permitted. This gives 57 such districts (44 had multi family housing). A visualisation is shown below

In the next step I plotted the stops using the 'routes' attribute, plotting all the stops led to a lot of overplotting, since I was potting the 0.5 mile buffer as well, and most of the districts shown here which were closer to Boston had a good coverage in terms of the stops. So I looked at only the Commuter Rail stops which were spread further away from Boston. This image helps see how many of the municipalities do not have coverage from the 0.5 mile buffer area around a transit stop, which would mean that they could not have atleast one district in that buffer zone. The image is shown below.

From the image it is clear that there are several municipalities which are not in the vicinity of these buffer zones. I visually look at them and group them into a set called 'violators'. This set contains 38 municipalities.

One effect of these municipalities not being near a transit station is that if people want to go to another municipality, they need to use their own vehicle for atleast part of the way, and I wanted to understand if the people living there had the income to afford a car. For this, I looked at the income of the households in these municipalities - and focused specifically on what percentage of households had the lowest listed income here - under $10000.

Framingham is the municipality which has the highest percentage of very low income households among the 'vioators'. This and municipalities like Peabody and Manchester would particularly benefit from being more connected to the MBTA transit routes, since the lower income households are less likely to be able to afford and use private vehicles.
